Who Am I?

I have a passion for learning and am constantly trying new things. Whether it is a new recipe, a new crochet or knitting pattern (I learned to knit this year through youtube and online tutorials!), a new computer program or figuring out why a line of code does what it does, I enjoy thinking things out.

More About Me

What sort of professional background do you have?

I graduated from Penn State University with a Bachelors of Science in Art Education. During my time at college, I focused on studio arts including graphic design. After graduating, I taught for a few years and even though I enjoyed the being with children I realized teaching was not for me. I started getting back into graphic design and began relearning the Adobe Creative Suite.

Most recently, I've been learning online through thinkful.com. I will be completing their frontend web development course within the next month. From there I plan to continue by taking Frontend AngularJS.

In 2010 I was hired by Penny-Dell Publications and learned Quark Xpress 6. I am a desktop publisher who designs both print and digital publications. I freelance on occasion for Digital Gravity Media, a SEO company based in Stamford, CT.

What is your dream job?

My dream job would be with a company where I could design, develop, maintain and update websites. I would want to be constantly challenged and given the opportunity to learn new scripting languages and programs.

What is your design style?

I think all design should be:

  • User friendly

    • Let's be honest, no one likes to be confused. User-friendly design is a must. If no one can understand it, no one will use it, read it, think about it

  • Simple

    • Simple and streamlined designs are a must.
    But design should also make you think. There is a reason we have brains, after all.

  • Satisfy our basic need to communicate

    • Imagine a world without paintings, sculptures, books, music; a world where no one communicates. A grey world without creativity, thought, wonder. Pretty boring, right?
    From cave paintings to digital media, we all just want to share our thoughts.
